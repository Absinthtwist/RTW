Day 98: Edinburgh To Reykjavik

Tuesday 12 June 2012

transition Edinburgh, Scotland to Reykjavik, Iceland

Today will be a little less fun than I had initially hoped, but I’d rather a lot of time spent reading in an airport than having to spend hundreds on a new ticket after I miss a flight, and I’m pretty sure I would. I’m awake by 0630, not by choice, my eyes just pop open. I hang out in the hostel common room and charge my phone as best I can, no plugs in my room. Eventually, daylight burning, I hit the streets and find a good and greasy British breakfast complete with blood pudding and the saltiest bacon I’ve ever had. I take my time with it, but it’s all too much for me, and I push the plate away unfinished.

I return to the bracing Scottish air and decide to just spend the money and be warm. It’s a good call, I’ll need that warmth in Iceland too. So I purchase a short red plaid scarf (Clan Wallace, apparently) and a charcoal jumper (sweater to my fellow yanks). I pay far too much, but the only currency more valuable than money is time, especially right now. I can’t spend an hour looking for a good deal, especially so close to tourist central.

So come 0945, jumper and scarf attired, I figure I have an hour at best. I walk across the bridge to explore a huge monument I saw previously, tall and pointed like the top of a cathedral, only larger. Closer inspection reveals this to be the Scott Monument. That stops me. I’ve seen a great many amazing monuments: to kings, to warriors, to saints, gods, but never to a writer. This just astounds me. What glorious and wonderful things does it say about a culture that prizes literature so highly? I must read up more about this. I’ve never read a word of Walter Scott, and suddenly I feel that I should.

Well, the time has come, and I grab my bus and ride to the airport, arriving 1135. I weigh my pack for curiosity, 13.9kg, that last 4kg is all souvenir crap. My pack and I have a seat and start reading, and my flight to London is without incident, wheels down at 1600. I have a good chunk of time on my hands here, so I grab a bite to eat, finish my book and start a new one, and finally am boarding at around 2100.

As we soar into the air, the sun has only recently set and the evening lights are twinkling as we cut through the clouds. In that twilight, watching the earth fall away, I am struck again by the magical impossibility of this act, of flying. It just seems unreal. As we rise over the clouds, the sun returns, hovering at the horizon in perpetual sunset for the remainder of the flight.I set my watch back an hour as we touch ground, 2330, and step out in front of the airport to watch ad the sun finally slips below the horizon just before midnight. I find my bus into town, but have to wait as it fills up then makes the thirty mile trip into Reykjavik. I am nodding off as we drive into town then from hotel to hotel, the post- sunset sky holding impossibly, like a musician’s high note, unwavering.

I am dropped off and checked in to my hostel and make it to bed around 0200, the curtain drawn on the perpetual twilight.

Tomorrow is a light schedule, just wander the town, see what comes up.

Safe journey.
