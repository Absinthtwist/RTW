Day 53: The Half Way Point

Saturday 28 April 2012

transition from Amman, Jordan to Luxor, Egypt

As my journey is projected at one hundred and six days, today marks my half way point. It is also my departure from Asia – for now – to the continent of Africa.

A rather straight forward day. Out of the hotel around 0900. Last drive around Amman before the airport. A quick flight over to Cairo. Cairo airport is large and modern and lovely… and empty. The few people walking its corridors only serve to emphasize its emptiness, like ghosts haunting its halls.

I make my connecting flight to Luxor and am met by the cab driver sent my my hotel. The ride into town is fantastic. On the west bank of the Nile, time has stopped. People are still living the same way as hundreds of years ago (with a few conspicuous exceptions). I saw a farmer working his field pulling a cart by animal power. The east bank, in comparison, is where you find the Hilton and the McDonalds.

My hotel, and I use this term in the broadest possible sense, seems quite cool. It is not so much a hotel as a permanent camp site. It is perhaps not luxurious but certainly comfortable, and more to the point, very welcoming. I am greeted by the owner who sits and talks with me about my plans here in Luxor, and insists that I make myself at home. The rooms are small bungalows, with shower and toilet a short walk away. My bungalow is made of strong reeds of some sort, like thin bamboo. On the inside the reeds are all horizontal, and I glimpse a layer behind that vertical. There is a ceiling fan, a mosquito net, and a firm but large bed. It will do nicely, I think.

Tomorrow I wake early, then off to see the Valley of the Kings! Hard to be blasé about that.

Safe journey!
