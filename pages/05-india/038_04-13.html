Day 38: Too Hot

Friday 13 April 2012

Varanasi, India

First really rough night of trip. The ceiling fan no doubt helped some, but still quite hot, and the bed is the hardest I have yet slept on, which is truly saying something. When my alarm goes off at 0500 I shut it off and try for more sleep. Knowing I’ve missed my morning window today, I sleep in and take my time. Tomorrow I simply must be up early, but today I take it easy. I hand wash yesterday’s clothes in the sink and hang them to dry. That done, I casually saunter out the door by 1100 or so.

Across the street from my hotel is a crumbling brick and white-wash building claiming to be a movie theatre. I have a look and see, with a grimace that not only are they showing an American film, but a truly bad looking one. Still, a movie experience with an audience full of Indians, tempting. And the thought that it is likely dubbed into Hindi does improve its appeal. Still, my trusty google map says there’s another theatre down the road a bit. Might be worth a look.

I make my way through the bustling streets until I see a place advertising itself in English as a bakery, restaurant, and shop. The bold English writing and the promise of wifi screams “tourists only please,” but screw it. And tourist in Varanasi means something different, I think.

I step inside and find two other tables filled, westerners of course. On one table is a somber and rather androgynous person going over her(?) various papers, writing. At another table sits a young European couple, finishing drinks and playing UNO. The menu has a variety of non-Indian foods, American, French, German. I even see a burger on the menu, thoughabsolutely not beef. And absolutely no Indian food on the menu. Rather than leave, I decide to stick it out and enjoy the relative cool shelter here for now. On a whim, I order nachos and a cheese sandwich. I must say the bread of the sandwich is fantastic, and the nachos surprisingly good. The salsa is impressive, and the chips, a fried flour tortilla rather than corn, make it interesting. I wash it down with a 7up and a liter of water for the road. The proprietor offers me tiramisu, but I’m quite full and leave well enough alone.

I remember once a friend of mine, not from America or Europe, who felt uncomfortable around dogs. He explained, very courteously, that where he comes from, dogs are perceived very differently. Looking out the window of my bakery retreat, I watch the street and see the occasional dog wander by. There are no leash laws in effect here in Varanasi. The dogs give every impression of living not just on the street but from the street. They root around for what scraps they can, wander where they like. Yesterday I had a friendly run-in with a dog, she came up to me as I sat on the steps of a ghat and we had a pleasant moment. But it occurred to me, even as I scratched behind her ears, that this is no domesticated American dog, with all her shots and I microchip. Her mood could turn in a dime and a bite could quickly lead to rabies. Shortly after, as if to reinforce the point, as I was walking I came across a pack of dogs all lying together. When I got a little too close, the alpha became very vocal regarding his displeasure, and would not silence until I was well and away. Here in the streets of India, dogs are wild animals. Point taken.

I finish my 7up and see where the road takes me. I have a general idea to look for that movie theatre, if I can find it. The other I found was not di obvious. As I approach, however, I am stunned by what I see. A mall. A glass and steel modern mall. Tiny by American standards, but here it looks like a space ship. I must investigate.

I have to go through a metal detector and a pat-down to enter. A bit funny seeing how loaded all my pockets are and I wasn’t required to show any of it. The mall was a bit unimpressive, one big room going up three stories, escalators, lukewarm AC (emphasis on warm), and a good three or four derelict storefronts. Attached from the outside is the promised movie theatre, with some better viewing options, but I’m not feeling it just now so I keep walking.

I wander the streets, cutting through a few alleys, which appear to be residential in nature. I discreetly make my way past and head back to my hotel room. Sleeping in seems a less intelligent choice just now. I would be wiser to wake early then nap through the worst heat of the day. Foolish of me to forget this. Tomorrow I need to check out of my hotel, so I lose my siesta option. I’m feeling dehydrated and worn down more than I would have expected. Guess its been a while since I lived in this sort of intense heat. I’m starting to rethink the early start tomorrow. No doubt dawn on the Ganges would be phenomenal, but I’m not feeling too great just now, and I don’t think anything can top my last Ganges experience. We’ll see.

With only a quick run to grab some good and four liters of water, I decide to just lay low. I love the streets, but the heat is profound. I spend the evening updating my website as best I can while watching Bollywood music videos. They are quite a cultural window.

Tomorrow I take the train to the sacred temples of Khajuraho. In the meantime, lots of water!

Safe journey!
