Day 39: Under The Weather

Saturday 14 April 2012

Varanasi, India

Another rough night. Hot as hell, upset stomach and nausea. A couple trips to the bathroom, but not as bad as could be. Checked out of hotel and by 1100 I’m back at the same bakery as yesterday. My train leaves at 1800 and I’ve decided to take today easy. Not entirely sure how to pull that off, here in Varanasi, but I’ll do what I can.

1300

Still in my bakery. Had crepes for breakfast, with a touch of lime and sugar. More water. Sipping 7up while I read more if my Gandhi book. My back is sore and I feel exhausted. Keep drinking water.

Feeling better after a warm meal and nearly two liters of fluid into me. I take a bicycle-ricksha to the train station. My first time on such a conveyance, and clearly it was made for smaller people than I. As we ride through the chaos, I can’t help thinking one good pothole and out I go. Butmy driver steers us right and without incident. As we approach the train station (which I did not see coming into town), the city displays a bit of a change. Fewer potholes and more paved roads. The buildings are taller, three sometimes four stories high. And more and better organized vendors.

As I wait for my train, I am treated to the view of monkeys, casually hanging out on the tracks. No one seems to care. By 1750 I’m seated in my train and ready to go. Looks reasonably comfortable. I got a main seat, not that crappy side seat like last time. I think that will make the difference. Not as full, either. Khajuraho strikes me as more of a tourist location, less traffic. Sounds backwards, but there’s a lot of that going around in India.
