Day 44: Bollywood!

Thursday 19 April 2012

Jaipur, India

Utterly lazy day. Crawl out of bed by 1100, amble out for some lunch. Hang out, take a few pics. At 1400, I decide I want to do something, and head out for a movie.

I find the Raj Mandir Cinema Hall. It looks nice enough from the outside, but I brace myself. Realistically, torn seats, dusty ceiling fans and stifling heat all seem about right. I get in line to buy my ticket. Two lines, one for ladies another for gents. There are three ticket classes, and I figure I’m probably better off going high-end. The fellow in front of me is maybe eighteen, and has the same idea. As those seats are sold in pairs, we team up. My new friend’s name is Krishna, and together we step in as I prepare for the worst.

I am stunned. Nothing could have prepared me for this. Its a palace. A quick check with Wikipedia shows that this place was built in the 1970s, but it is beautifully maintained and immaculate. The seats seem pretty standard, with my premium seats at about what most Americans are used to these days. I’m in the balcony, and have a good uninstructed view at the isle.

The lights dim, the movie starts. It’s all in Hindi with a few words of english here and there, but aside from nuance, I follow it pretty well. The film is called Housefull 2, and is a romantic comedy with concealed identities, mistaken identities, all sorts of loops and twists, to get the girls. Four bachelors, four lovely ladies. And of course, there is singing and dancing, which is infectiously enjoyable. The film is just under two and a half hours, and includes an intermission. I exit the theatre, losing Krishna in the crowd, and head back. Have a peek at the trailer.

I’m still not feeling 100%, and I think a lot of that is my anxiety regarding Indian sanitation and my general mistrust for every drop of liquid ormorsel of food I consume. But I do feel I’m getting the hang of India, though far later than other countries yet visited, and with only a few days left before I depart. But that sanitation issue remains a block, irresolvable. I find that it is such a fundamental thing, how do you have a nuclear arsenal and put satellites in space when people have shit in their streets? Is it poverty? Is it cultural? I can’t seem to get past it.

Train for New Delhi tomorrow at dawn, I think I’m about ready. Gonna sign out early for tonight, I’ll let you know if I get up to any real fun mischief.

Safe journey.
