Day 42: Sleeper Trains Suck

Tuesday 17 April 2012

Transition from Khajuraho to Jaipur, India

Today is officially day forty-two but it still feels like forty-one. It’s 0230 in the morning as I step off the train in Agra. Agra is certainly on my must-see list, Taj Mahal, but that comes later. This is just a layover. In a clever trick I came up from the comfort of my bedroom in the states, I ride the train till two am, then wait three hours and take a taxi across town to connect with another train. Sigh… See, the idea in my head was that the sleeper train gave me more time. These rides are twelve hours and longer. A day, gone. The idea in my head was that I’d be ok sleeping on a train, that it has this romantic air about it. Ok, good learning experience, healthy sleep is far more important. So my Agra to Delhi run is a night train, maybe I can change that. I also bought a similar train ticket from Luxor to Cairo which is meant to be delivered to my hotel in Luxor. If its there, fine. If not, Divine intervention.

1100

approaching Jaipur. First impression, more modern. Cleaner. Glimpsed a camel being led down the street. As we get deeper into town things get a bit messier.

As soon as I get off the train I am approached asking if I need transport to a hotel. I’m getting kinda used to this, so I give him the name of my hotel and we agree on a price. I follow the man out and am stunned not to find another auto- rickshaw but an actual car, with AC. The driver and I set off, and we converse about my plans here in Jaipur. He gives me his business card which lists many of the top sights to see, and is quite willing to drive me around. I’m getting the feeling the pickup from the train station is less about the fare as it is about a tour job. Well, he seems a right enough guy, and his English is good, so I agree. I tell him I’m dead tired and need a nap and we decide he’ll pick me up at 1600.

The drive through town confirms my initial impressions via train. Quite a different city, Jaipur. We even pass a KFC. The colonel is the real US ambassador to the world. The traffic signs are more or less obeyed. The streets are kinda clean. The gain smell of sewage still persists, but only distantly. There is clearly more of a tourist presence here, and I am reminded that I am now in the Golden Triangle. This is the big triumvirateof tourist cities in India of New Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur. It’s still a far cry from Disneyland, but it’s a whole lot more tourist friendly than Varanasi.

The hotel room almost makes me cry. The AC is frigid and the bed so soft and comfy, when I sit for a sec to try it, it’s hard not to fall asleep. I quickly hand-wash some clothes in the sink, I’m still uncomfortable turning my laundry over to anyone else to wash, in any country. Here, where replacement clothes may be impossible given my height and frame, perhaps a little more so. After washing what I can quickly, I fall into bed and right to sleep.

When my alarm goes off at 1530 I know I’ll be useless for sightseeing, so come 1600 I’m downstairs making my apologies and we set for 0900 tomorrow. Here’s to banishing cynicism and that this driver is really and nice as he seems. Most people I have talked to in India have been genuinely hospitable.

I sleep for a wee bit, hand-wash another bit of clothes, then, realizing I have not eaten in twenty-four hours, decide to be silly and treat myself to the Colonel’s eleven herbs and spices. It’s a short walk, and I order a three piece chicken meal. Of the various images in the dining area, the one the strikes me is an image of two very buff Indian men arm-wrestling. I remember reading in my Gandhi autobiography how when he was young, late nineteenth century, there was an argument around India that the reason the British were so successful was because they ate meat. It made them strong and healthy. Gandhi had been raised in a culture where vegetarianism was the practice. This idea that meat eating equals macho strength is a curiosity to me. Is this image here on this wall a glimpse into how that thinking is still, on some level, present? The vast majority of Indians I see on the street are very thin. Of course, then I turn on Bollywood TV and wonder who the fuck those people are, because they sure as hell don’t look Indian to me. Dark hair and accents, sure. But beautiful fair skin, and many of the male stars are built like pro-wrestlers. Now, I do see a few more athletic Indians, but those people on TV are just…alien. It unnerves me a bit.

They sell a skin cream to make your skin lighter, lots of commercials for that. Gotta say, coming from the States, where we have our own race issues, seeing people pay money to fake a skin color freaks me out a bit. Of course, we have fake tans, but I don’t dig those either. It’s just bizarre to me.

Anyway, I only manage to eat the one piece of chicken. The way I’ve been going the past few days, small wonder my stomach has shrunk a bit, and no harm there. I wrap up the other two pieces and head back home for a big fat nothing and some sleep.

Long-ass day. I am content to have waived off the sight-seeing till tomorrow. Sleeper trains are an epic fail, I must say, though I am glad have had the experience. Tomorrow I’m going to see if I can arrange more sensible transport to Delhi.

Safe Journey!
