Day 47: A Pleasant Day

Sunday 22 April 2012

New Delhi, India

Last full day in Delhi, though I still have my day trip to Agra. Took me a while to get to sleep last night, no AC in this room, and I felt a little off when I dragged myself out of bed. It was a relaxed morning, leisurely shower and breakfast, then off by 1100.

First stop, chosen by my guide was another swami temple. More down to earth than yesterday’s, and a little nicer for it in my humble opinion. Nothing stunning, but nice. And of course, no photos.

We then made the drive to the main attraction of the day, Qutub Minar. Mughal Empire again, once a great mosque with a fabulous tower reaching into the sky. The mosque is in ruins, rather beautiful ruins, but the tower remains beautifully intact (or well repaired at least).

We make a stop at the Railway Museum, which is clearly aimed more for locals that travelers like me, and rather young locals at that. But it is charming, with many preserved train cars and a charming museum exhibit. It’s a light day, so I take my time.

Lastly, we stop by the India National Museum. It’s a pretty good museum, though a bit hot and stuffy. I enjoy the galleries and exhibits, but between you and me, you can see better stuff in New York or London. A shame, but there it Kali, Goddess of Power is, what can you do? Even the Elgin Marble is in London. And let’s not let Paris off the list either. Daylight robbery. Saraswati, Goddess of Education And really, that’s about it. I wander through the markets, enjoy some truly amazing tikka masala with the dusk.
