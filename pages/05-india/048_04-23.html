Day 48: Last Day In India

Monday 23 April 2012

New Delhi, with a side trip to Agra, India

Awoke earlier than I’d like and got a ride across town to the train station. Ironic in that I’m a block away from the biggest train station in Delhi, but haven’t set foot in there. Wrong station for my needs. The train ride is adequate enough, I can’t complain, and I’m in Agra arranging a ride by 1130.

By noon, in the dusty, unforgiving heat, I arrive at one of the most iconic sights on earth. The Taj Mahal Some things don’t quite live up to their hype, others surpass them. The Taj Mahal is breath-taking. All of the complex surrounding it seem to focus the experience. And I must say, the people of Agra tend to take their world heritage responsibilities seriously: Agra and the surrounding area is free of factories and industries. Just tourism and crafts. That precious white marble needs to be protected. They even make all the visitors put little disposable booties over their shoes just like OR. It was hard to pull myself away, but eventually, it’s time to move on.

Having the driver for the day and plenty of time to kill, I am taken to see some of the local crafts, very beautiful, and am even treated to a musical demonstration, a private concert just for me of drums and sitar. It felt a little awkward being the only audience, knowing I wasn’t gonna drop the two grand US for a nice new sitar, but I settle into it and accept it for what it is: hospitality.

From here I am taken to Agra Fort. Made by the Mughals around the same time as the Red Fort and Amber Fort I’ve already seen (as well as the Taj Mahal), I’m not certain how thrilling this will be, but I am pleasantly surprised. The other two forts, particularly the Red Fort in Delhi, have been pretty beat up. The red fort was all but leveled by the British following an Indian rebellion in the 1850s, and this fort is in wonderful condition. I can imagine the Mughal maharaja leaning over his balcony down the river to the awesome view of the Taj. It’s a gorgeous image.

With still a bit of a wait but already having spent way too much money, I return to the train station to chill for a bit. I won’t really have time to sleep before catching my plane, so today shall be very long indeed.

My train to Delhi is delayed, and I make to my hotel by midnight.
