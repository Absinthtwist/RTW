Day 73: Athens

Friday 18 May 2012

transition from Heraklion, Greece to Jerusalem, Israel

Not much of a blog entry for today. I slept in. Fully charged my camera and phone, keeping it plugged it till the last minute. I really felt completely done with Greece and was just ready to go. It’s nothing on Greece, it’s been amazing, it’s like this every time: when I know it’s time to go, then I’m just ready to go. I’m done. So I chill in the hotel till check out time and am on the street by noon. The airport is only two or three kilometers away, so I start walking. I figure I’ll grab some food on the way.

As it happens, none of the food stands or restaurants I passed seemed very inspiring to me, so I just kept walking and grabbed some chow at the airport. My hopes of picking up a book to read dashed, I used the airport wifi to download the Anansi Boys by Neil Gaiman. Not exactly a sequel to American Gods, but certainly a part of the same world. The book holds my attention while I wait for my flight to Athens, and while I wait for my flight to Tel Aviv.

The flights were both without incident, and I pull in to Tel Aviv after midnight. Which means……to be continued.

Safe journey.
