Day 76: There Is Always Something You Can Do

Monday 21 May 2012

Jerusalem, Israel

I am up early today, ready for Bethlehem. I arrive at my pick up point, a hotel about a kilometer away, a few minutes prior to pick up time, 0830, but see nothing that looks like a tour group. I wait it out till 0900 and throw in the towel. I write an email to the tour company to see if anything comes, but with a trip this complex, you gotta expect a few lumps. I ponder for a bit on what I should do, and by 0945 I am on the road and walking to Yad Vashem, the Holocaust Memorial Museum. It’s around five or six kilometers, and I decide it would make a nice tour of New Jerusalem.

Alas that much of this route is uphill, but it’s still a very pleasant walk. By 1115 I am at my destination, atop a hill surrounded by forest. It is very peaceful, very beautiful. Winded, I make my way to the cafeteria and have a croissant and a sprite. I see they have two cafeterias, with no overlap permitted: a dairy area and a meat area. Naturally, kosher laws would be well enforced here.

Rested, I rent an audio tour and proceed. This is one of those things that nothing I say will adequately express what I experience here. So much, and so perfectly done. I cannot imagine how this could have been done better. I don’t think I want to give a blow by blow, just know that it was deeply moving, extremely informative, profoundly engaging. I lost track of time, and I was overwhelmed.

Adjoining the museum is a garden memorializing all the non-Jews who risked their own lives to help. And most powerful of all, a memorial to the one and a half million children who were murdered in the Holocaust. The memorial was underground, in the dark. A room seemingly made of glass all around you, and one candle in the center, reflected a thousand thousand times, like standing in a star filled sky… as the names and ages of the lost are read aloud. My description is crude and clumsy, but this memorial is anything but. Everyone should stand here. Everyone should see this. Everyone should hear this. Everyone should feel this. So much that could have been will never be. So much, lost.

Jerusalem has been a very overwhelming destination for me. And more than once I have felt that I have been given more than I know how to accept. But I keep trying, I keep trying. Jerusalem is ABSOLUTELY on the willing to see again list. And if I thought they would let me live here, I’d start learning Hebrew tomorrow.

I think I can say that this has been the best part of my journey so far.

Safe journey.
