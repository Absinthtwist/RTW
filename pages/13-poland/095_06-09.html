Day 95: To Cracow

Saturday 9 June 2012

transition from Paris, France to Cracow, Poland

I’m not entirely sure how long it will take to get to the airport, or really even how to get there, so I am up early and out the door by 0700 just to be safe. Turns out there’s a train going right to the airport, no problem, and I am there by 0800. Paris is well organized and I figured it would be like this, but I hate the thought f missing a plane, and I tend to be over-cautious. I sit and read for a while, an enjoyable rest, and by 0935 I am in line to check in. More waiting, and more waiting still, and finally, by 1115 we are finally boarding. This flight is through easyJet, one of the cheap airlines, and they give no seat assignments. Whatever, just get me there.

Wheels down in Cracow at 1330. No immigration inspection, presumably as I’m coming from inside Europe, so I just walk right out and find a bus into town. It gets me kinda close, and I walk the remainder. I’m walking in to the old city center, still feeling quite medieval, and is damn charming. I find my hostel and drop off my bag at 1530, then go walk around the old city. There’s a wide square filled with people. Musicians ply their trade, locals dressed up posing for pictures. A fantastic old church. Even a Hard Rock Cafe. I am reminded that not so long ago this was all on the wrong side of the Iron Curtain. Nothing says the fall of an oppressive communist regime like a Hard Rock Cafe.

Down the road and up a hill I find a castle which I explore a bit, and another beautiful church. Within are monument tombs to sixteenth century rulers I have never heard of, and I am again struck by how little I know of Poland, and how everything here is a delightful mystery.

By 1800 I stop and have a rest at a cafe in the old town square. I keep my meal simple, Paris was a budget catastrophe but one I accepted. Mostly I am here for the free wifi. I update my phone and upload a few more pics to my website, then pretty much call it a night. A little extra sleep sounds fantastic, and I should wake early tomorrow anyway, I’m expecting complications with the tour I arranged from the states. Poor communication.

Safe journey.
