Day Twelve: Relaxed

Sunday 18 March 2012

Kyoto, Japan

1200: Starbucks

Slow start today, more relaxed. Took a long shower, walked to the main rail station and picked up my Japan Rail pass. Japan is one of my more expensive stops, and, owing to ATM withdrawal limits, I was not able to pull out all the cash I would need here. No big, right? Well, as it turns out, few, and I mean very few ATMs in Japan accept American debit cards. So today I am questing for the right machine. It’s a bit of a project, and just now I am stopping for a relaxing beverage. And of course, my favorite sport, people watching. More later.

1915

Bit of a relaxed day today. Went on a quest for an ATM, resolved. Turns out post offices here have what I was looking for. Who knew. I went back into the train station, figuring I’d ride out to Arashiyama, but Sunday, even in Japan, means limited service. After waiting a bit with no luck, I decided to try another train. I had my Japan Rail pass and was curious to take a ride. I considered taking the train to Nara a day early, but on the last minute I grabbed a mystery train before it pulled from the station. I spent about 40 minutes riding through the town before getting off the train and heading back. At a point more or less near my hostel I got off the train and started walking. Sometimes people ask what I did or saw today, and it’s moments like this that are so very rewarding, but hard to explain. I just wandered around. Not exactly lost, you can’t really be lost with GPS in your pocket, but I certainly had no idea where I was. Traveling like this lets you discover the unexpected, and more important, you soak up the feel of a place. I ended up strolling through the Gion district, the old Kyoto, and to the Yasaka Jinja temple, which is the temple I was at last night, full of life and food stalls. I grabbed some chicken tempura and went to Gion Corner for a show demonstrating classical Japanese culture including the tea ceremony, flower arrangement, dance, and an interesting version of puppets. The dancing in particular was beautiful, with Geishas in full regalia. Graceful, elegant, feminine, beautiful. I made it back to the hostel to charge up the batteries and to continue the never ending quest to upload my photos. Today was a landmark: I finally finished uploading my China pictures. Soon I should be able to upload pics to the website. Off to bed now. Safe journey.
