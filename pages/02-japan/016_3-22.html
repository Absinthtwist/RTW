Day 16: Tokyo Disneyland

Thursday 22 March 2012

Tokyo, Japan

A travel day. My flight leaves at 0840 and the nice folks at my hostel insisted that I should leave leave in plenty of time, four hours before my flight. Knowing what I kn3w of Beijing traffic I can see their point, but at0400 the roads were bare. So here I am well before take-off, well before even check-in, and nothing for it but to wait.

Slept in and took our time, made it out the door by 1000. We took our time, stopped for breakfast, got a little lost with the metro trains, but made it into Disneyland by noon. There are two parks here, Disneyland and Disney Sea. We decided to focus on Disneyland, though Disney Sea looked a lot cooler than it initially sounded. A giant volcano towers as their centerpiece, and it looks like they have both the submarine ride and the Indiana Jones ride over there. Alas, would probably have been cool.

On the way to the Disneyland park we were seduced by the beauty of the Disneyland Hotel, and we began to hatch a very irresponsible and luxurious plan. We looked into getting a room at the Disneyland Hotel, despite the ludicrous price, but had no luck. Either they were out of room or it was a communication problem, hard to tell. The Japanese people are so obsessed with being polite that they won’t tell you no even when appropriate, they won’t even tell you they don’t understand you. It can be a little frustrating, but their courtesy is still impressive.

So after that delay, we went into the park itself. As discussed before, I’m quite familiar with the original Disneyland, and am acutely aware of every similarity and difference.Instead of Main Street USA you have the World Bazaar, which was nearly identical to Main Street. Two immediate differences: first, the whole street is covered over, giving a sort of Art Nouveau World Expo kinda feel. Then second is the absence of a train. I did find a Disneyland Railroad in Adventure Land, that did its own self contained loop with no stops, but no train station, no tunnels to pass through under the tracks.

While the park was quite large, transitions from one land to another often seemed quite sudden and unexpected. World Bazaar would quickly turn into Adventure Land which quickly melted into Western Land.

This Being a cloudy weekday, I think we both were caught off guard by the large crowds. Our first ride, after walking around for a bit, was the Haunted Mansion, with a two hour wait.

Very similar to the California original, with the amusing exception of the Japanese language. Hearing Donald Duck or Winnie the Pooh talk in Japanese is quite entertaining. At this point, we both agreed that we wanted to take our time and Linda was willing to be insanely extravagant and pay for a room. After making some inquiries, we secured a reservation. Not at the Disneyland Hotel, but at the slightly less expensive but still Disney Ambassador Hotel, which had a wonderful, elegant Art Deco motif.

I took one look at the swimming pool and couldn’t help but imagine Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers dancing across the steps. We settled into our room, relaxed a bit, then went back into Disneyland for more fun. I was surprised and pleased to discover that the old retired Disneyland classic the (Main Street) Electrical Parade is alive and well in Tokyo, both with classic floats like Pete’s Dragon and new magic like Mike and Sully!

We played into the night, staying late enough for fireworks, but early enough to make in back to our room for our 90 minute professional massages. Very luxurious, not cheap. I am living so modestly for the rest of the trip, I kinda just said “Fuck It.” A Comfy bed, a fantastic shower, it was wonderful. The massage finished, we both slept like rocks.Safe Journey….. zzzz
