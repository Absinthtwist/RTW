Day 17: More Disneyland and Shibuya

Friday 23 March 2012

Tokyo, Japan

A travel day. My flight leaves at 0840 and the nice folks at my hostel insisted that I should leave leave in plenty of time, four hours before my flight. Knowing what I kn3w of Beijing traffic I can see their point, but at0400 the roads were bare. So here I am well before take-off, well before even check-in, and nothing for it but to wait.

While we wanted to continue our theme of luxury and decadence, staying in the hotel earned us a pass into the park fifteen minutes earlier, so that was hard to say no to. To that end, we woke early and had room service delivered. Very posh having the room service, but the food was kinda bad. Japanese people mean really well when they try to cook western food, but more often than not its a fail. Pancakes and an omelette with bacon, all pretty off. But still, rolled into our room. Very cool. Alas, our plan to get into the park early didn’t quite work, checking out of the hotel took longer than expected. Further, we asked at the desk if they were able to mail packages to the States, and like I said before, the Japanese hate saying no or saying that they don’t understand, so we killed a lot of time waiting before we gave up and headed into the park.

Even at opening time the crowd was crazy. And fanatical: they were running, many of them full speed. You’d think that someone was gonna loose the dogs or something. At first this seemed crazy, but when I saw the line for Thunder Mountain, a quarter of a mile away from the ride, never mind all the ropes and switchbacks, I was stunned. We moved on to Splash Mountain.

With some patience and the use of Fastpass, we got in the rides we most wanted to see. We did have to let a few go. It was stunning to see two hour plus lines for some rides, then fifteen minute waits for rides like Star Tours and Pirates of the Caribbean.

Linda commented, and rightly I think, how animatronics, state of the art 1965, just no longer impresses, especially here in Japan. I would have thought that name value alone would keep these rides busy, but apparently not.

By about 1500 we knew we had done out best and had to look forward. Tomorrow we had to fly to Cambodia and did not wish to carry more thanwe had to with us. Few post offices were open at this hour, and there would be no time in the morning. So armed with that knowledge, we gathered ourselves up, said our good-byes to the Magic Kingdom and rode back into Tokyo, to the Shibuya.

The Shibuya might be compared to New York’s Times Square, full of lights and action at any hour, and a post office open into the night. We made our way through the rain and sent our packages off and took our time heading back to the train station and back to the hotel.

At the hotel, we did one load of laundry, packed ourselves up for the coming day, then fell into the best sleep manageable on such a hard bed. And really, aside from that high priced Ambassador Hotel, this bed is the softest I’ve had since leaving the states. Oh well. Here’s to hoping good things for Cambodia. Safe journey!
