Day 29: A Farewell to Elephants and a Return to Bangkok

Wednesday 4 April 2012

transition from Ayutthaya to Bangkok, Thailand

Up early and mucking out the elephants’s pens. It’s quite a mess, and hard work, but quite satisfying. And I confess I also felt a bit wistful: this will be my last time, unless I am able to return, and who knows when that may be.

By the final ride of the day, I feel like I am beginning to get a handle for this, and alas, it is time to give Honey one last affectionate pat then say goodbye. Also a bit sad is the parting of new friends. Through the magic of the Internet it is hoped that ties may hold over distance, but the road ever cries forward.

It’s a long car ride back to Bangkok, shared with my new friend and fellow elephant handler, Leah. We chat a bit, but soon slip into quiet: it’s been a very full few days. We are both dropped off at Khao San Road, get ourselves checked in to our respective accommodations, then we sit down for a relaxing bite to eat. We step on to the street to have a look at the truly over- stimulating Khao San, but agree we are both worn out from the day and part company. I wander through the maze of shops and stalls perhaps another thirty minutes then return home for laundry and sleep.

Tomorrow Bangkok!

Safe journey!
