Day Six

Monday 12 March 2012

Xi'an, China

Awoke around 0600 this morning, but stayed in bed until around 0800. Had a pleasant breakfast and am now waiting as my clothes are in the washing machine. A nice lady with very little English was kind enough to help me figure the machine out. It’s hard to keep up with the charging up of batteries through all of this. I knew it would be a challenge, and there are certainly worse things. Having my iPhone with me is a huge help. I have two things I need to do today, and one thing I’d like to do. Of the things I need to do, one is laundry and that is happening right now. The second thing is that train ticket. I paid for a train ticket online to be delivered to my hostel, but no show. I will need to buy something today, hopefully there is something available today, or tomorrow at the least. If pressed, I can fly, but I was really hoping for the train. If push comes to shove, I don’t need to be in Beijing until Thursday morning, though that would really and truly suck.

As to the thing I don’t need to do but would really like to do, I would very much like to investigate the wall that surrounds the old part of Xi’an. It’s the only complete city wall in China, hundreds of years old, and is about 10 miles all the way around. It looked cool as hell as I passed in yesterday.

Thankfully, the train ticket was delivered right as I was checking out of the hostel. With that done, I walked straight to the city wall. It took a little exploring before I could find an official entrance, but it was worth the effort. 40 feet tall, 40 feet or wider at the top, 60 feet at the base, covering an 8 mile square wall around the original city. The only intact city wall in China, and one of the oldest such walls in the world. Not too bad. You can rent a bicycle and ride the loop, but time being limited, I made my way to the train station.

The Xi’an Railway Station was amazing. A crazy insane ant-hill meets feeding frenzy filled with more people that I would think possible. If course, all of that chaos was banished by my first class ticket as I was let into the nicer, more elite waiting room. I should mention that much to my surprise almost everyone I meet has at least some amount of English. I really did not expect that. As for the train itself, I shared a compartment with three other people. We each had a bunk, two upper and two lower. I read for a bit, then it was lights out for all as we each got some version of sleep as the train bumped and rattled on through the night for the 14 hour ride.
