Day Ten: Beijing to Kyoto

Friday 16 March 2012

transition from Beijing, China to Kyoto, Japan

0430

A travel day. My flight leaves at 0840 and the nice folks at my hostel insisted that I should leave leave in plenty of time, four hours before my flight. Knowing what I know of Beijing traffic I can see their point, but at0400 the roads were bare. So here I am well before take-off, well before even check-in, and nothing for it but to wait.

While there are many wonders of Beijing that I shall miss, the air quality is not among them. Dry sooty air, constant nasal congestion and mild eye irritation. This city is hemmed in on three sides by mountains. Fantastic for Pre-Industrial military fortification, not so much for post industrial smog control. They’ve cleared the factories out of the city, but there are still so many cars and coal fires.

I’d also like to take a moment and compare my two mainland Chinese cities, Hong Kong is in a class by itself. Xi’an was enjoyable, but I would not seek to return. While parts of it were charming, much of the city is best described as “bombed-out” looking, like the site of an air raid. Buildings crumbling, filth and grime all too common. On more than one occasion I saw someone, in the middle of town, step behind a bush to empty a bladder. Some of this I just accepted as cultural difference, but Beijing was quite different. Perhaps it is just their tourist show-piece, but it was much cleaner. Less bombed-out. Some of that, I suppose, was from a general Pre- Olympic house cleaning. Both cities are seeing a significant building boom, I’m curious how they shall appear in a decade.

Oh, side note: I asked about the rare food items. I was told there is one market that sells all the crazy stuff, grilled grasshopper, what have you. I was further informed that very very few locals have any interest, mostly that stuff is for the tourists. I saw plenty of seafood on menus, beef, chicken, pork, of course Peking Duck, but nothing more exotic than that. While outside of Xi’an, near the Terracotta Warriors, I saw many vendors in the markets selling many things. A few of them sold rather disturbing looking animal furs, and I don’t mean mink. Think domestic. I declined to investigate further at the time.

1230: Busan International Airport, South Korea

Three hour layover, waiting for flight to Osaka. It was such a pleasure to step into the restroom here and see it and smell it so immaculately clean. I don’t think I realized how rough China was until I got here. Hard to make a real sense of a place from an airport, but my initial impression is that I like South Korea is love to see more of this country, but it will have to be another occasion.

Had a little trouble with my ticket getting out of Beijing, had to make an international call to my travel agent. In retrospect it probably wasn’t anything serious, but it gives me more confidence in my agent, which is good.

Right now I am enjoying a super healthy lunch of Pringles and Gatorade while watching a Korean reality show with singing. Had a cucumber sandwich and rice porridge on the plane. Gonna be a bit of a wait.

1820: Kansai International Airport, Osaka, Japan

Flight went well. I’m excited to be in Japan. I’ve felt a kinship with this culture since going to grade school in Gardena, surrounded by Japanese kids. My friends were Japanese. Many of the toys we played with were Japanese toys. Unlike China, where I knew exactly two words, I know… well, not very much Japanese, but more than two words. Perhaps as many as twenty or thirty words, which is a respectable start. I am currently sitting in the van waiting for my ride from Osaka to kyoto, which I imagine will be about two hours. Sigh. At least it’s better than a four month tall sailing ship cruise. Hmmm… That actually sounds awesome, never mind.
