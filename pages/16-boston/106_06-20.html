Day 106: The Last, The Longest

Wednesday 20 June 2012

Boston, United States of America

So this is the last day of my journey. I return to Spokane to visit family for a few days, then back to Seattle and real life. It will be nice to rest in Spokane, but that is no journey. Today is my last travel day. And quite by chance, today is also the summer solstice, the longest day of the year. Quite auspicious, no doubt. And also by chance, today will be quite a long flight day. Not the longest, but still, eight and a half hours in the air, give or take.

Up and out of the hostel by 0510, we retrace our steps and walk back to the bus/train station we were first dropped off at upon arriving in Boston. A short wait later we cram in like proverbial sardines beyond what I’d imagine this bus could actually carry, stuffed like something you’d see in the third world, then we are off to the airport.

The security screening seems less organized and more annoying than I’m used to, and I find the removing of my shoes to just be funny. But by 0650 we are at our gate, and by 0740 we are on the plane. Been far too long since I’ve seen that sort of perfect efficiency.

We land in San Francisco at 1100. The flight was long but I’ve had worse and can’t really complain. We maneuver through the terminal, find our gate and have a seat. I can feel the impatience welling up in me, I just want to get going and be done. After an agonizingly long thirty minutes or so, we board without incident. My bag, which has always been carried on with only one exception, a tiny jet in Cambodia, is too small for the overhead bin. Try as I might to stuff, it shall not go. My impatience is ramping up, and I am about to surrender to having to check my bag. I take a seat, just to clear the isle and let pass the boarding passengers, and just to get it out of the way, I put my bag at my feet and shove it half under the seat. Good enough. I put my feet on top of my bag and the flight attendant never gives it a second look. So be it.

We seem to taxi forever, but eventually we do lift into the air, and I feel relief. It is done, the last flight is away. The flight is about ninety minutes, I can deal with that. The battery on my iPhone is wasting away, but I say screw it and play away, both mindless solitaire and music, luxuries I tend to ration, but no point now. At last, without fanfare or drama, the plane touches down. I am one of the first out the door, pack over my shoulder, and I head outside to my ride home.

And that’s pretty much that.
