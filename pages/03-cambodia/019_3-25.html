Day 19: Angkor Wat

Sunday 25 March 2012

Siem Reap, Cambodia

Slept in till about 0830 and headed downstairs to join Linda who had a head start on breakfast. We agree that when we go to Sihanoukville on Wednesday, taking a plane is preferable to the bus. Big difference in price, but it’s one hour vs ten hours. We also arrange for a private tour guide and tuk-tuk driver to take us to our reason for being here: Angkor Wat.

Our driver arrives on time and we are on our way by 1030. It’s already around 90f, cool for this time of year, and rather humid. I decided to wear sandals today, shoes seem less practical in a country where you remove them often, and I figure it might be a good change for my feet. I regret that choice within a few hours, but it is what it is.

I must make clear that this really and truly is the jungle. This giant complex, covering many square miles, was completely lost and forgotten for centuries. How is that possible?

The jungle. It’s been cleared back and some restoration has been done, but make no mistake: these are ruins. Truly amazing, beyond description, breath taking ruins. Straight out of an Indiana Jones movie. At different points in Angkor Wat’s history, it’s focus has shifted between Hinduism and Buddhism, though the original design was Hindu and the current slant is Buddhist. There are great long walls covered with intricate relief carvings depicting the epic stories of Rama and Krishna, and there are many many seated Buddhas, nearly all without heads. The heads wereremoved by treasure hunters through the years, quick profit and easier than carrying away the whole thing.

After a few hours, we retreated to a nearby open-air restaurant of sorts, directly across from Angkor Wat. This place was recommended by our tour guide, and it was amazing.

Linda and I each had a fresh mango shake, and we each had a local chicken dish. Mine was a curry, I’m not sure what hers was, but they were both delicious. Not spicy like Thai food, but a similar experience in some ways. Worn out by the heat, we decide to take it easy, and at our request our guide takes us to an artisans’ workshop to look at local works. Truly beautiful, and I spend far more than I should, then I spend almost as much again to have it shipped home.

A bit foolish and impulsive, but done is done. We were then taken to a reputable massage establishment, where we both got a one hour body massage. Being rather massage-naive, I was intrigued by the difference between my Japan massage and this one. I liked this one better, but it was less soft. It was rough, push and pull and contort. Alas that my dear friend Linda, who has had back problems in the past, seems to have tweaked something and is in some pain afterwards.

We head back to our hotel and try to rest her back and my feet, and a couple hours later, as the sun is setting, we decide to walk a bit. A local French restaurant is recommended to us but we fail to find it, and instead end up in a rather sub- par and out of place “Irish Pub.” I’m not exactly sure what makes it Irish, probably not the bad RnB cover music we are subjected to, no nor the menu. Best not to ask, I suppose. We walk back to our hotel through the busy streets, dodging tuk-tuks and motorcycles and enjoy a drink on the balcony before calling it a night.

Tomorrow, better shoes for me, and an earlier start with less sun, hopefully. There’s a great deal of Angkor Wat and it’s surroundings to explore.

Safe journey.
