Day 25: Phnom Penh

Saturday 31 March 2012

Sihanoukville tp Phnom Penh, Cambodia

The bus to Phnom Penh, really, was nothing like I had feared. We were picked up in a van at 0930 and taken to the bus, parked in an alley. We hopped on with our luggage and were driven to a bus station where we changed to a different bus.

This bus was reasonably comfortable, had something resembling air conditioning, and was moderately smooth on the road. Linda and I stretched out in different rows for more comfort. I think Linda actually had some thought that the four and a half hour drive time we were quoted would be accurate. I on the other hand have begun to see how things work around here and was ecstatic when we actually left on time, and not a bit shocked when we stepped off the bus in Phnom Penh six hours after starting.

We grabbed a tuk-tuk and were off to my hostel, checked in with luggage secured. We grabbed a bite to eat there in the hostel and I was finally able to scratch my itch for western food with an actual pizza. I know, it sounds terrible of me, all this distance and I want American food. Hey, it’s a comfort thing. I think I’m good for a little while.

We stretched our legs a bit around a temple and a market area, both pleasant but neither stunning. The immaculate ribbon of park along side the river was also quite pleasant.

By this time the sky was darkening, and Linda decided to leave early for her plane. We parted ways with a big hug, then off she went in the tuk-tuk.

Back to being solo again. I love Linda dearly, but I am glad. I am ready to be alone again. Tomorrow I head to Bangkok.

Safe journey.
