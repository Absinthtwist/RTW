Day 22: Siem Reap to Sihanoukville

Wednesday 28 March 2012

Siem Reap to Sihanoukville, Cambodia

We tried to sleep in today, but we were both awake and up by 0730. We packed up our things and went downstairs for our last breakfast in Siem Reap.

I feel pretty done here, ready to move on. I’m a bit surprised that three days can actually feel adequate. Mind, I could spend more time here, still so much to see. But I am ready to move on.

1030

Got our tickets and waiting for plane. They said our bags were too small to carry on, and as we were both taken by surprise by that, we neglected to lock our bags up. Linda is a bit nervous as she has all her money in that bag. Oh well, done is done. We can hope everyone is good and honest here. Or at least that they don’t find anything they want to take.

1850

We arrived on time and were out of the airport and in a van by 1300. Took the drive to the beach and were let off to walk the rest. I got us walking the wrong direction but we eventually found our place. A bungalow on a hill on the beach. Perhaps three stories of stairs away from the water.

We drop off our stuff, make sure the A/C is good then we are off to find food. We see a place saying it has burgers and pizza and are tempted. We order a couple drinks – my piña colada tastes ok but us not frozen – and when the pizza comes it is quite a lesson. Funky thick bread and bizarre tomato sauce and a few bits of some unknown melted cheese. Quite interesting, but not pizza.

We walk a bit, we lay out on beach chairs a bit. The beach here is filled with beach chairs and umbrellas. The narrow walkway lined with thatch roofed bars. Poverty is here too, with many people walking from place to place,selling their wares. Manicures and sunglasses appear to be big, though I find my attention grabbed by the squid- kabob lady. My favorite, of course, is the lady balancing a large dish of fresh fruit on her head as she walks about her way. We also see a number of amputees and persons with birth defects hoping for money. It’s hard: either you toughen up or get eaten alive. The decision to help or to keep walking is a difficult one, and quite personal.

I’m laying quite luxuriously in a hammock on the porch of my bungalow, in the dark, looking down at the lights of the bar down by the water as I wait for Linda to return from yet another massage. She said this was she intended to get a massage nearly every day while on this vacation, and she has kept her word. I can hear the unmistakable crackle of a fire from down below, and music. And of course, above it all, the haunting roll of the surf and the call of crickets. This is very beautiful. It will be hard for me to do nothing, but it really does seem to be the thing to do at this moment. I don’t expect my reports will be terribly interesting over the next couple days. Sorry, but that’s how it goes. I know I’m having a great time. here, still so much to see. But I am ready to move on. Bangkok next.

Safe journey.
