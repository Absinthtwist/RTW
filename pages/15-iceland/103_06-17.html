Day 103: The Land Of The Free

Sunday 17 June 2012

transition from Reykjavik, Iceland to Boston, United States of America

I wake and shower, and am checked out of the hostel by 0900. I hang out in the common area for the time being, emptying my pack and reloading it carefully. I am stuffed to the brim with things I have purchased, but see nopoint in mailing them at this late hour. I relax and do a little reading, aside from travel, I intend to keep today light.

I head out around 1200 and grab some pizza, really very good, then walk through the center of town which is surprisingly crowded. Every now and then I’ll see a woman or a girl in traditional dress, and there are Icelandic flags flying about. I make a quick stop to secure my ride to the airport and ask what the occasion is. Today, it would appear, is the Icelandic Independence Day. What a treat! I walk around the crowded square a bit, stopping for a coffee, then catch my 1430 bus and am on my way.

I reach the airport to check in by 1530 and secure a boarding pass but with no seat assignment: the plane is full and I am on stand-by. This makes me nervous, but the man at the desk assures me I’ll be on the plane. Just for curiosity I weigh my pack, which comes in at 15.5kg, nearly 6kg of souvenirs and other crap. I walk to the gate and fish my way through the crowds. I sit and wait a bit. When I stand, a stranger taps me and points to my wallet on the floor near where I was sitting. People can be cool, quite often from what I’ve seen.

I finally secure a boarding pass with a seat and relax. I don’t mind not being the first on the plane, so long as I have a reserved seat. It takes them longer than expected to let people board, and at one point I do believe they were actually taking bags off the plane. We are, however, eventually let to board and all is well. My seat is right at the exit if the plane. First to disembark and with six feet of leg room. I can’t complain. As the plane takes off, I suddenly wonder if the baggage ever got put back on the plane, and I feel quite grateful that I don’t check my bag.

On the flight I watch a movie, “The Way” with Martin Sheen, seems appropriate. About a man walking the Camino, to Santiago de Compostela. It’s a pilgrim trail one walks, across northern Spain mostly, though people often start in France. Walking typically takes a month or more. Quite a good film. And again, it seemed appropriate, especially as my journey is coming toward its end. One more city.

Wheels down by 1900. I am to meet my friend, Tom, who is also flying in to Logan. We’re to hang out in Boston and catch a Red Sox game in Fenway. There’s a little confusion on my part on which plane he’s on, I thought hewas meant to arrive before me, but by 2030 it’s all sorted out and we are heading by bus toward downtown and our hostel.

The bus ride is a straight-forward, and we walk the last half-mile or so. It’s nice to be back in a big city, one where everyone speaks English. We drop our bags off at the hostel and grab a late dinner around 2200, a couple of sandwiches from Panera, and call it a night. Tomorrow: Freedom Trail.

Safe journey.
