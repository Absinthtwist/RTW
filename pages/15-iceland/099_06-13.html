Day 99: A Light Day

Wednesday 13 June 2012

Reykjavik, Iceland

I’m awake and up by 0730, despite my intentions to sleep in. I start my day once again with laundry, and finally, by 1055, I am out the door.

Reykjavik is a mellow town of less than two hundred thousand. Of course, all of Iceland has three hundred and twenty thousand people, that’s like one city block in India. Incidentally, the Indian Embassy is across the street from my hostel in what can only be called a strip mall. Things are a little more laid back here.

Reykjavik has its charm, to be sure, but it’s not the city that captures one’s eye, it’s the bright blue sky, the deep indigo of the bay, and the rocky hills at the horizon. The architecture is pragmatic, sensible, with a few stand-out moments. There’s a cathedral with a great tower, looks to have been built in the twentieth century, a bit of a rocket-ship appearance to it. The people, as if answering all my best hopes, all carry a hint of that Viking blood, healthy and active in appearance. The locals are dressed in tee shirts and perhaps even shorts, while we tourists bundle in coats against the 45 f weather. And the sound of the Icelandic language is fantastic: anything said by anyone seems to carry images of coastal raids and horned helms (at least to me).I stop for a bite to eat and a bit stunned by the island prices. I was warned that most travelers would do best to stick with fast food, and I understand now. All the same, I order a bowl of goulash, a soup of lamb and vegetables and rice in a broth served with a slice of delicious bread. But yeah, I see fast food a lot in my future for the next couple days. Oh well.

I keeping walking through the town until I reach the Perlan at 1400, a museum of sorts, made with life-size recreations and scenes with headphone explanations imparting a basic history of Iceland up to around the sixteenth century. It’s pleasantly entertaining and informative, certainly more than I knew coming in, and excellently done. The potential with this setup to be profoundly cheesy is always high, but with a dash of wit and good theatre, they pull it off. I would say its over-priced, but everything is over-priced here, so it doesn’t particularly stand out.

I head generally back toward the center of town, toward the bay. After a few disasters with a small compass (that I no longer carry), I started using shadows to give a general sense of direction when walking. Rules are a bit different up here in Iceland. Shadows that should be pointing mostly east are point almost directly north. Good thing I know where I am. I make it to my own neighborhood at around 1530 and find that my super-expensive soup has already worn off. I accept my fate and stop for a passable soft taco before deciding to just hang out and charge everything up. Tomorrow my Iceland adventure begins in earnest.

Safe journey.
