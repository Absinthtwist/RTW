Day 56: Karnak Temple

Tuesday 1 May 2012

Luxor, Egypt

Lazy morning again, had trouble sleeping in the dusty unrelenting heat. And of course there are the bugs. My insect repellant spilled on a flight and I never thought to replace it. Now my arms read like brail. Anyway, out the door before 0900 and ferried across the Nile. A two or three kilometer walk up-river to Karnak Temple.

Yesterday I saw Luxor Temple, which was amazing. After Karnak, however, Luxor Temple is best described as “cute.” Karnak is amazing. And huge. Supposedly the largest ancient religious site in the world, and only perhaps a third is open to the public. Many obelisks, including a fallen one, lying horizontal. A vast hall of towering Herculean columns, spread out like a forest, supporting no visible roof, profoundly impressive. Every square inch of wall is packed with great pictographs and hieroglyphs. In some areas, one can see bits of faded color, a glimpse of what this entire place must have looked thousands of years ago, splashed with vivid hues. As I walk between the towering columns, all I can think is that this place was ancient beyond words when Julius Caesar walked here with Cleopatra. And still, here it stands.

After Karnak I stop for a bite to eat and make my way back to camp. I relax and chat with my fellow travelers for a bit, step out for a lite dinner on the river bank, and generally try to relax, avoid the heat and the bugs.

Around 2130 my four British friends and fellow travelers, all set to ride the same train. We take a boat across the water, and make our way around Luxor Temple one last time, then up the road to the station. The train station looks new and quite nice, and when the train comes it seems quite pleasant. I say farewell to my companions and make my way to my train car.

Unlike India where compartments were separated by curtain, this is more like China. Each compartment has a door, and inside there are two bunks, top and bottom. I take the top bunk for security, though as it turns out I have this compartment to myself. The bed is a bit short for me but adequately comfortable. I am prepared to settle in for the night when, to my surprise, I am brought a chicken dinner. I do what I can with that, saving a roll and an orange for breakfast, then try to get some sleep. This is by far the bumpiest of all the trains I’ve been on, but I think I manage to doze off a little. To be continued.

Safe journey.
