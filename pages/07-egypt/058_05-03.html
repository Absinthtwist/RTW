Day 58: Politics

Thursday 3 May 2012

Cairo, Egypt

Not much to say about today. Yesterday, while I was out walking, nothing particular to be seen or heard, at least ten human lives were ended in conflict between police and protestors. It’s the sort of thing I was conscious of as I approached Egypt, not wanting to take stupid risks. Things seemed reasonable enough, and Luxor was just fine. No politics whatever from that quarter. The. This shit.

To be honest, it does not make me scared, it makes me angry. I find it especially annoying as I saw no protests or rallies of any kind. Am I at risk for this nonsense every time I walk down the street? I accept a certain amount of risk, but not stupid risk. There’s a lot of amazing stuff to see in Cairo, and maybe I’ll look back with regret, but the call I’m making today is to just lay low. I’m gonna blow off most of my sight seeing in Cairo and justchill in my hotel rooms. Short of open armed conflict, I feel I really should go see the pyramids and the sphinx, and being so far removed from the center of town I’m not too concerned there. As for the rest of the sights of Cairo, it’s a shame but I’m writing them off one and all. Just gonna chill. It makes me angry. But it is what it is.

Tomorrow, Friday, is the holy day here. I may do the pyramids tomorrow, or if that strikes out, then Saturday. Come Sunday, off to stable and peaceful Istanbul.

Safe journey.
